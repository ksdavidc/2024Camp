Longest living things

Longest-living land mammal – Human beings

Jeanne Calment pictured in 1995, at the age of 120.

Longest-living aquatic creature – Glass sponge

Bolosoma stalked glass sponges (Hexactinellid)s are one of the longest-living organisms on Earth. Despite their delicate appearance, it is estimated that they can live up to 15,000 years.

Longest-living bird – Cookie the cockatoo

The longest-living bird on record is Cookie, a male pink cockatoo (Cacatua leadbeateri), who lived in captivity at Brookfield Zoo, Chicago, USA. Incredibly, Cookie outlived the next longest-living pink cockatoo by 52 years.

Longest-living rodent – Naked mole rat

The naked mole rat can survive for 18 minutes without oxygen and is also resistant to many types of cancer. In 2020, it was reported that one male individual turned 37 years old, which makes it easily the longest-living rodent on Earth.

Longest-living invertebrate – Ocean quahog

The oldest ocean quahog, thought to be over 500 years old and known as 'Ming'.

Longest-living tortoise – Aldabra giant tortoise

Adwaita the giant Aldabra tortoise resting inside its cage at the Alipore Zoological Garden in Kolkata, India,was thought to be 255 years old when he died at Alipore Zoological Garden, Kolkata, India in 2006. Adwaita was thought to have hatched in 1750 and had lived a solitary life at the zoo for over 100 years.

Longest-living dog – Bobi

Bobi, a purebred Rafeiro do Alentejo dog hit an incredible 31 years of age before shuffling off this mortal coil in October 2023. Rafeiro do Alentejos are native to Portugal and are known to be calm in nature and make great guard dogs.
